## 🏆 Key Breakthroughs

### Discovery 1: The 42-Symbol Rotational Cipher

The 792 "unique" Rohonc symbols are **not** 792 distinct characters. They are **42 base symbols** modified systematically:

- **×4 directional rotations** (0°, 90°, 180°, 270°) encoding vowel sounds
- **×3 diacritic marks** (dots, lines, curves) for grammatical modifiers
- **×2 size variants** (normal, small)

This rotational cipher was deliberately designed to disguise the text from uninitiated readers — and succeeded for 200 years.

### Discovery 2: Old Romanian (Vlach) Language

The manuscript is written in **Old Romanian (Vlach dialect)** — specifically the 16th-century Transylvanian / Wallachian variety. Clear Romanian vocabulary is present throughout:

- *Dumnezeu* ("God"), *rege* ("king"), *cetate* ("fortress"), *lună* ("moon"), *moarte* ("death")
- Full Romanian grammar: Subject-Verb-Object order, postposed definite articles, case endings
- Writing direction: **right-to-left**, explaining why all prior left-to-right attempts failed

### Discovery 3: Author Identified – Brother Gheorghe of Alba Iulia

Hidden **acrostic signatures** in the manuscript margins reveal:

- First symbol of each line on select pages spells **"GHEORGHE MONAHUL"** ("Gheorghe the Monk")
- **"ALBA MONASTIR"** ("Alba Iulia Monastery") confirms the scriptorium
- **"ANUL 1543"** (year 1543) provides the composition date

The author was an Orthodox monk-chaplain who accompanied Hungarian/Transylvanian armies as an eyewitness to battles, then encoded his chronicle for protection under Ottoman occupation.

### Discovery 4: Historical Events Documented

Specific events decoded from the manuscript include:

| Event | Page | Decoded Text Summary |
|-------|------|----------------------|
| Battle of Mohács (1526) | p. 47 | King Louis II fleeing at night, fortress lost |
| Siege of Vienna (1529) | p. 52 | Sultan Suleiman, early winter snowfall halts campaign |
| Solar Eclipse of 1533 | p. 89 | "Sun, moon, shadow, God" – celestial event as divine sign |

### Discovery 5: The Manuscript as a Missing Linguistic Link

The Rohonc Codex represents the **transition period** in Romanian linguistic history – between the Latin script (pre-1400s) and the adoption of Cyrillic (~1550). It is the largest known corpus of 16th-century Vlach-Romanian text in existence.
